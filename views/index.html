The World Needs a New Live Index

Yes, not one that gets updated every biyearly, if we're lucky.

As the world moves forward ever so quickly, we can now access a lot more information, and we now have the ability to make the most out of this information.

So, in fulfillment of our Technology and Markets class at New York University, we have decided to build a Global Live Happiness Index to measure the happiness of people in the world, one country at a time.

Come, discover more!

Measuring What Counts

Searching Globally, Understanding Locally

Whereas other indices use overly complicated algorithms to figure out the world's happiness, we decided to just use straightforward way: to see people in the world in their daily lives as expressed through social media, to figure out how happy countries really are.

We harness the power of social media in retrieving our data.We make our data even more accurate by using tweets, which are up to a certain extent, made without bias towards research. We scrape the internet constantly, and we get the number of happy and sad people, and that's what we give to you. This number is global and live. No time to waiting.

We also get to the bottom of what makes people happy by providing the most recent pictures of what both happiness and sadness means through Instagram. We're able to get the 20 most recent pictures of happiness and sadness around the world.

All in all, what we want to do is provide the world with the chance to see what happiness is globally and but see it with a local perspective. And that's what we bring to the table.
